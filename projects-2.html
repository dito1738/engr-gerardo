The Headphone Hook Upgrade

Project Scope
  • Goal: Declutter Desk Space
  • Method: Remixing & Parametric CAD
  • Date: Spring 2026
Tech Stack
  • Tinkercad
  • OnShape (CAD)
  • PrusaSlicer

1. The "Daily Annoyance"

I identified a recurring problem in my daily routine: my headphones were constantly cluttering my desk or falling on the floor because they did not have a place to naturally rest.

To solve this, I applied what we were taught about Systematic Inventive Thinking (SIT), more specifically Task Unification. My goal was to create a device that uses the desk itself to hold the headphones, without needing screws or permanent adhesive.

Initial Concept Sketch

2. Phase 1: The Tinkercad Remix

I started by finding existing 3D models of clamps and hooks. I imported these into Tinkercad to create a quick "looks-like" prototype.

Process & Challenges

I used Tinkercad's boolean operations (grouping holes and solids) to combine the mesh of a standard clamp with a hook geometry. This first iteration helped me understand the sizing requirements for my desk.

3. Phase 2: The OnShape Upgrade

After testing the Tinkercad print, I realized I needed a stronger, more precise mechanism. I moved to OnShape to use parametric modeling.

Why OnShape?

Unlike Tinkercad's drag-and-drop mesh editing, OnShape allowed me to define exact dimensions for the clamping screw.

4. Slicing & Printing

Print Settings
  • Printer: Prusa MINI+
  • Material: PLA
  • Supports: No (Designed for printability)
  • Infill: 15% (Grid)

I sliced the model using PrusaSlicer. I oriented the part to minimize supports and ensure the clamp screw threads were printed vertically for strength.

5. Reflection: Tinkercad vs. OnShape

Comparing the two tools, I found that:

  • Tinkercad was excellent for quickly bashing together the idea to see if the hook shape looked right.
  • OnShape was necessary for the mechanical parts (the screw and clamp) because I needed precise tolerances so the parts would fit together.

Did I solve the annoyance?

Yes. The final print successfully holds my headphones off the desk. If I were to do this again, I would increase the thickness of the screw handle for better grip.
