The Headphone Hook Upgrade

Project Scope
  • Goal: Declutter Desk Space
  • Method: TinkerCad Remixing & OnShape Design
  • Date: Spring 2026
Tech Stack
  • Tinkercad
  • OnShape (CAD)
  • PrusaSlicer

The "Daily Annoyance"

I identified a recurring problem in my daily routine: my headphones were constantly cluttering my desk or my room in general because they had no dedicated place.

To solve this, I applied the Task Unification pattern. My goal was to use a clamping mechanism to hold the headphones anywhere on my desk and any size desk I am working on.

Figure 1: Initial Concept Sketch

Design Thinking

Before using CAD, I sketched the what I had in mind to map out what I needed:

  • Surface Area: Holding my headphones with ease, while not taking up too much space.
  • Universal Fit: Ensuring the final print fits most desks and the mechanism worked.

Phase 1: The Tinkercad Remix

I started by trying to make my own clamp system by finding the screw shape(hole) and grouping it to a rectangle shape to create a hole within the rectangle. The manual way took too long and I could not the proper way to continue. I then asked the professor for guidance and he said that I could just find the models online and remix those. Wiht that new information, I went on to find some existing 3D models of clamps and hooks. I then imported what I found these into Tinkercad to create a quick prototype along with some designs of my own to find the best orienation.

Process & Challenges

The hardest challenge for this phase was finding the right way to attach the clamp and the hook. Right before printing I realized that I needed to equally change the sizes of the clamp mechanism if I changed the size of the hook. Luckily I made that realization before committing to a print, but I believe that even if I printed it wrong it would have been a well taught lesson in changing things properly. With that in mind, I moved on to the OnShape Upgrade.

Phase 2: The OnShape Upgrade

After testing the Tinkercad print, I realized I needed to make the clamp base and width larger because it was was too fragile. The more I tightened the bolt the more the print would bend. I then exported the file from TinkerCad to OnShape in order to make some improvements.

OnShape Process

Unlike Tinkercad's drag-and-drop editing, OnShape allowed me to define exact dimensions for the clamping screw along with more detaile sections It was tough at first, but the headphone hook model was not too difficult to improve upon given the tools from OnShape. Luckily the first tutorial we had to watch about making the tray introduced me to the right tools I needed to make the imrpovements for the final design.

The tutorial for the OnShape tray can be access here.

Slicing & Printing

Print Settings
  • Printer: Prusa MINI+
  • Material: PLA
  • Supports: No (Designed for printability)
  • Infill: 15% (Grid)

I sliced the model using PrusaSlicer. I oriented the part to minimize supports and ensure the clamp screw threads were printed vertically for strength.

Reflection

The final print successfully holds my headphones off the desk and the final orientation works with the . If I were to do this again, I would find way to make it less clunky and simplify the entire model Overall, this particular project taught me a lot of patience when it comes to making sure everytyhing is close to 100% before printing to minimze the time wasted. However, I did learn that it is okay if the print does not come out perfect because all that is called a learning opportunity. I was glad it only took me max one print before making the final print after and have it work the way I intended.
